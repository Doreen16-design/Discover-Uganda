Culture & City

Kampala: seven hills, one heartbeat

Built across seven green hills, Uganda's capital moves to the sound of boda-bodas, open-air markets and Afrobeat spilling out of rooftop bars. It's the easy, energetic counterpoint to the quiet of the parks — and the starting point for almost every safari in the country.

Beyond the capital, Uganda's 50-plus ethnic groups keep distinct languages, kingdoms and traditions alive — from the Baganda royal drums to the cattle-keeping Karamojong of the northeast.

FIELD NOTES — 03
01

When to go

Dry seasons (Dec–Feb & Jun–Aug) are best for gorilla trekking and game drives; roads are firmer and forests less muddy.

02

Entry point

Most journeys begin at Entebbe International Airport, a short hop across Lake Victoria from Kampala.

03

Gorilla permits

Permits are limited and sell out early — book through the Uganda Wildlife Authority months ahead of a trek.

04

Getting around

A 4WD with driver-guide is the standard way to cover the distances between parks on Uganda's murram roads.
